# Draft Mutual Confidentiality Agreement

> **Legal-review warning:** This is a general transaction template, not legal advice and not a signed agreement. The parties should complete all bracketed fields and have qualified counsel review the final document, especially governing law, remedies, compelled disclosure, residual knowledge, and international data-transfer issues.

This Mutual Confidentiality Agreement (the **Agreement**) is entered into as of **[Effective Date]** by and between:

- **David Turner**, an individual located in Virginia (**Seller**); and
- **[Buyer Legal Name]**, a **[jurisdiction and entity type]** with an address at **[address]** (**Buyer**).

Seller and Buyer are each a **Party** and together the **Parties**.

## 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to evaluate a possible acquisition of the WeaveStudio software source code and associated intellectual-property assets (the **Purpose**). Each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the other solely for the Purpose.

## 2. Confidential Information

**Confidential Information** means nonpublic information disclosed by or on behalf of a Party (the **Disclosing Party**) to the other Party (the **Receiving Party**) in written, electronic, visual, oral, or other form that:

1. is marked or identified as confidential; or
2. reasonably should be understood as confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure.

Confidential Information may include nonpublic source code, architecture, tests, build and deployment information, product plans, business information, pricing and negotiation terms, diligence materials, security information, credentials inadvertently disclosed, and the existence or status of acquisition discussions.

## 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information the Receiving Party can document:

1. was lawfully known without confidentiality restriction before disclosure;
2. becomes public through no breach of this Agreement;
3. is received lawfully from a third party without confidentiality duty;
4. is independently developed without use of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information; or
5. is approved for release in writing by the Disclosing Party.

## 4. Use and protection

The Receiving Party will:

1. use Confidential Information only for the Purpose;
2. protect it using at least reasonable care and no less care than it uses for its own similarly sensitive information;
3. disclose it only to employees, officers, professional advisers, contractors, financing sources, and representatives who need it for the Purpose and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement;
4. remain responsible for breaches by its representatives to the extent permitted by applicable law; and
5. not reverse engineer, decompile, copy, publish, distribute, commercialize, or use disclosed source or product materials except as reasonably necessary to evaluate the proposed transaction.

## 5. Security and credentials

Neither Party authorizes the disclosure of passwords, private keys, recovery codes, API secrets, personal account credentials, or unnecessary personal information. If the Receiving Party discovers such material in a disclosure, it will stop using it, notify the Disclosing Party promptly, and securely delete or return it as directed.

## 6. Required disclosure

If the Receiving Party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information, it may do so only to the extent legally required and, when legally permitted, will:

1. provide prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party;
2. reasonably cooperate, at the Disclosing Party's expense, with efforts to seek protective treatment; and
3. disclose only the portion legally required.

## 7. Return or destruction

Upon written request or termination of discussions, the Receiving Party will within **[10] business days** return or securely destroy Confidential Information, including copies, except that:

- routine backup copies may be retained until automatically deleted under normal retention processes; and
- one archival copy may be retained by legal counsel solely for compliance and dispute records.

Any retained copy remains subject to this Agreement.

## 8. No license or obligation

All Confidential Information remains the Disclosing Party's property. No intellectual-property license, ownership transfer, partnership, agency, employment relationship, exclusivity obligation, or commitment to complete a transaction is created by this Agreement.

Either Party may stop discussions at any time unless a later signed agreement states otherwise.

## 9. Accuracy and reliance

Confidential Information is provided for evaluation and may be incomplete or subject to correction. Neither Party makes a representation or warranty concerning Confidential Information except as expressly stated in a later signed definitive agreement.

## 10. Term

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date. The duty to protect Confidential Information continues for **[three] years** after the last disclosure, except that source code, credentials, and legally protectable trade secrets will be protected for as long as they remain confidential or receive protection under applicable law.

## 11. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that unauthorized use or disclosure may cause harm not fully compensable by money damages. Subject to applicable law, the Disclosing Party may seek appropriate equitable relief in addition to other available remedies. This section does not predetermine liability, damages, venue, or entitlement to relief.

## 12. No public announcement

Neither Party will publicly announce the discussions, proposed transaction, price, or the other Party's identity without prior written consent, except when legally required.

## 13. General terms

1. **Governing law.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of **[State/Country]**, without regard to conflict-of-law rules.
2. **Venue.** The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction and venue in **[courts and location]**, unless they agree to arbitration in writing.
3. **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other Party's written consent, except to a successor in a merger or sale of substantially all relevant assets that assumes this Agreement.
4. **Notices.** Notices must be sent to the email and physical addresses listed below or later designated in writing.
5. **Entire agreement.** This Agreement is the complete agreement concerning confidentiality for the Purpose and supersedes prior confidentiality understandings concerning the same Purpose.
6. **Amendments and waivers.** Amendments and waivers must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.
7. **Severability.** If a provision is unenforceable, it will be modified to the minimum extent necessary, and the remaining provisions will continue.
8. **Counterparts and electronic signatures.** The Agreement may be signed in counterparts and electronically, each treated as an original.
